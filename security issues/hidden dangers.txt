# Hidden Dangers in Computer Networks

While computer networks offer powerful benefits such as communication, resource sharing, and Internet access, they also pose **hidden dangers** that can compromise security, privacy, and reliability.

---

## Common Hidden Dangers

1. **Data Theft**
   - Hackers can steal confidential information like passwords, financial details, or private files.

2. **Unauthorized Access**
   - Intruders can gain access to systems or networks without permission.

3. **Viruses and Malware**
   - Harmful programs such as worms, trojans, and ransomware can spread through networks.

4. **Phishing Attacks**
   - Fake emails or websites are used to trick users into sharing sensitive information.

5. **Denial of Service (DoS) Attacks**
   - Networks or servers are overloaded to make services unavailable for genuine users.

6. **Eavesdropping**
   - Unauthorized interception of data transmission (e.g., Wi-Fi sniffing).

7. **Identity Theft**
   - Using someone elseâ€™s identity to commit fraud or access resources.

8. **Loss of Privacy**
   - Tracking of personal information, habits, and communications.

---

## Conclusion
Hidden dangers in networks can cause severe financial, personal, and organizational damage.  
**Security measures** such as firewalls, encryption, authentication, and monitoring are essential to mitigate these risks.
