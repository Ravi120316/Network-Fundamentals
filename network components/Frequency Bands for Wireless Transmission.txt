# Frequency Bands for Wireless Transmission

Wireless communication uses radio waves that are divided into different frequency bands, each serving specific purposes.

## 1. Radio Frequency (RF) Band
- **Range:** 3 kHz – 300 MHz
- **Uses:** AM/FM radio, maritime communication, walkie-talkies
- **Note:** Supports long distance but low data rate

## 2. Microwave Band
- **Range:** 300 MHz – 300 GHz
- **Uses:** Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, microwave links, radar
- **Note:** Shorter wavelength, higher data transfer speed

## 3. Infrared (IR) Band
- **Range:** 300 GHz – 430 THz
- **Uses:** Remote controls, short-range communication
- **Note:** Requires line of sight (LOS)

## 4. Visible Light Band
- **Range:** 430 THz – 770 THz
- **Uses:** Li-Fi, optical communication
- **Note:** Extremely high speed, requires LOS

## Special Frequency Bands for Wireless Communication
- **2.4 GHz Band (ISM):**
  - Common for Wi-Fi (802.11b/g/n), Bluetooth, microwave ovens
  - Longer range, more interference
- **5 GHz Band:**
  - Used in modern Wi-Fi (802.11ac/ax)
  - Faster speed, less interference, shorter range
- **60 GHz Band (Millimeter Waves):**
  - Used in 5G and high-speed short-range communication

## Summary
- **RF:** Radio, long distance, low speed
- **Microwave:** Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, radar
- **Infrared:** Remote controls, short range
- **Visible Light:** Li-Fi, very high speed
- **2.4 & 5 GHz Bands:** Common Wi-Fi and Bluetooth frequencies
